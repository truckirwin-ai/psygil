# Psygil — 60-Second Demo Script

**Format:** Screen recording with voiceover
**Target audience:** Forensic and clinical psychologists
**Goal:** Show the report-writing pain point being solved, not a feature tour

---

## The Script

### COLD OPEN [0:00–0:03]

*Screen: The Psygil IDE, dark theme. A competency evaluation case is open at the Review stage. The case tree is fully populated — intake, testing, interviews, diagnostics, all visible. No narration. Let the density of the interface register.*

**Visual note:** The case tree alone tells the story — this is a tool built for serious clinical work, not a consumer app.

---

### THE PAIN [0:03–0:08]

*Hard cut to: A blinking cursor in Microsoft Word. Empty document. The filename in the title bar reads something like "Jones_CST_Report_Draft.docx".*

**VOICEOVER:**
> "You just finished a competency evaluation. Twelve hours of interviews, testing, collateral review. Now you're staring at a blank page."

*Hold on the blinking cursor for a beat. Every provider watching just felt that.*

---

### THE SOLVE [0:08–0:18]

*Cut back to Psygil. The clinician clicks "Generate Draft." Brief animation showing the Writing Assistant pulling from every stage — test scores, interview notes, clinical observations, the clinician's own diagnostic decisions. The report populates in the editor pane.*

**VOICEOVER:**
> "Psygil already has everything. Your intake. Your test data. Your interview notes. Your diagnoses. It writes the first draft in minutes — not hours."

**Visual note:** The report appearing in the editor should feel fast but not instant. Two to three seconds of visible generation conveys "the AI is doing real work," not "it's a template."

---

### YOUR JUDGMENT, YOUR REPORT [0:18–0:28]

*The editor now shows a complete forensic report — proper sections visible as the camera scrolls: Referral Information, Background History, Behavioral Observations, Test Results, Clinical Impressions, Forensic Opinion. The clinician highlights a sentence in the Clinical Impressions section, makes an edit.*

*Quick cut to the Diagnostics tab: the evidence map where the clinician individually selected each diagnosis. No "Accept All" button visible anywhere.*

**VOICEOVER:**
> "Every clinical judgment in this report is yours. You reviewed the evidence. You selected each diagnosis. The AI organized your work — it never made a clinical decision for you."

**Visual note:** The absence of "Accept All" is the selling point. Linger on the individual diagnosis selections for a beat. The providers who care about their work will notice.

---

### THE PIPELINE [0:28–0:38]

*Pull back to the case list. Six stages visible in the pipeline column — cases scattered across Onboarding, Testing, Interview, Diagnostics, Review, Complete. Click a case in Onboarding: the intake form appears. Click one in Testing: the test battery with scores. The stage-appropriate documents sell the workflow without a single word of explanation.*

**VOICEOVER:**
> "Track every evaluation from referral to archive. Always know exactly where each case stands — and what's left to do."

**Visual note:** Keep the clicks fast. Three cases, three stages, two seconds each. The viewer should feel the breadth without getting a tutorial.

---

### THE SECURITY BEAT [0:38–0:48]

*Show the status bar at the bottom of the IDE: "PHI: UNID Redaction ✓" and "Storage: Encrypted (Local)." Then a brief, clean graphic (not a screen recording) illustrating the concept: patient name replaced with a temporary code before AI processing, then restored after.*

**VOICEOVER:**
> "Your patient data never leaves your machine unprotected. When AI processes case material, names and identifiers are replaced with temporary codes — destroyed after every operation. The final report contains full PHI, as it should. It lives in your encrypted local database. Not in the cloud."

**Visual note:** This is the line that keeps the HIPAA-anxious provider from closing the tab. The word "local" does heavy lifting. Say it clearly.

---

### THE IDENTITY [0:48–0:55]

*Pull back to the full three-column IDE. Dark theme, dense, professional. Cases on the left, documents in the middle, editor on the right. A slow zoom out that lets the viewer take in the whole workspace.*

**VOICEOVER:**
> "Built for forensic psychologists. Not adapted from a medical notes tool. Not a chatbot with a clipboard."

**Visual note:** This is the "I want that" moment. The interface should look like something a serious professional uses — closer to a Bloomberg terminal or a coding IDE than to a health app.

---

### CLOSE [0:55–1:00]

*Fade to black. Logo appears. Tagline. URL.*

**ON SCREEN:**

> **Psygil**
>
> You diagnose. We handle the rest.
>
> psygil.com/waitlist

*Hold for three seconds. End.*

## Production Notes

### What to record first

The screen recording can be done entirely from the v4 prototype. Every interaction described above already works in the current HTML file — case selection, stage-appropriate documents, the editor, the diagnostics tab, the status bar. No development needed before shooting.

### What needs to be created

- The Microsoft Word "blank page" shot (5 seconds of screen recording, trivial)
- The UNID redaction graphic (a simple motion graphic showing name → temporary code → name restored, 3–4 seconds)
- Logo animation and end card
- Voiceover recording

### Audio direction

Male or female voice, but not a "commercial voice." Think NPR or a conference keynote — warm, confident, unhurried. The script is 148 words at the pace described, which is comfortable for 60 seconds with the visual pauses built in.

### What this script does NOT do

- **No feature list.** Features are shown, not listed.
- **No pricing.** This is awareness, not conversion.
- **No "powered by AI" hero moment.** The AI is positioned as infrastructure, not the product. The clinician is the hero.
- **No competitor comparison.** The "not a chatbot with a clipboard" line does that work implicitly.

### Why this order works

The script follows a problem → solution → proof → trust → identity arc:

1. **Problem** (blank Word doc) — emotional resonance
2. **Solution** (draft generation) — the "how" behind the promise
3. **Proof** (diagnostics tab, pipeline) — depth and clinical rigor
4. **Trust** (UNID, encryption, local) — removes the objection
5. **Identity** (full IDE shot, tagline) — positions the product in the viewer's mind

The report comes first because that's where the pain lives. Everything else is context that makes the report generation credible. A provider who sees the report draft will watch the rest. A provider who sees a feature tour will check out at second fifteen.
