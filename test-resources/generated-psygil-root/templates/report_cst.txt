MENTAL STATUS EXAMINATION
-------------------------
Appearance, behavior, and cooperation; speech and language; mood and affect; thought process and content; perception; cognition and orientation; insight and judgment.

TEST RESULTS
------------
Standardized instruments administered during the evaluation are summarized in the Test Results section of the report. All measures are scored and interpreted by the examiner.

FUNCTIONAL ABILITIES ASSESSMENT
-------------------------------
Factual understanding of the proceedings, including the roles of courtroom personnel, the adversarial nature of the process, the range of pleas available, and the potential consequences of conviction.
Rational understanding of the proceedings, including the ability to appreciate how the proceedings apply to the defendant and to apply factual knowledge to the defendant's own case.
Capacity to consult with counsel, including the ability to disclose relevant information, track and respond to questions, tolerate the stress of proceedings, and make reasoned decisions about plea and defense strategy.

CLINICAL FORMULATION
--------------------
Integration of background, behavioral observations, test results, and functional assessment findings into a diagnostic and causal formulation. Any diagnosis offered is the examiner's own determination based on DSM-5-TR criteria. This section identifies the mental disease or defect, if any, and explains the relationship between the clinical picture and the defendant's competency-relevant abilities.
