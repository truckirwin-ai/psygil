PROCEDURES
----------
The following procedures were used in the preparation of this report:
Review of records provided by the referring party
Clinical interview with the evaluee
Mental status examination
Collateral interview(s) where appropriate
Standardized psychological testing (see Test Results)
Symptom validity and effort testing
Administration of structured ADHD interview (e.g., DIVA-5 or CAADID)
Administration of validated ADHD self-report and observer-report scales
Cognitive testing with attention to attentional and executive function measures

Childhood school records and report cards, where available
Prior psychological and psychiatric records
Academic accommodation records
Medication trials and treatment notes

---------------------
Pregnancy, birth, and early developmental milestones
Childhood behavior at home and school
Symptom onset before age 12, with specific corroborating details

CURRENT PRESENTATION
--------------------
Current symptoms of inattention
Current symptoms of hyperactivity and impulsivity
Symptom presence in two or more settings (home, work, school, social)
Impact on academic, occupational, or social functioning
Duration of current symptoms

MENTAL STATUS EXAMINATION
-------------------------
Appearance, behavior, cooperation, speech, mood, affect, thought process and content, perception, cognition, insight, and judgment.

TEST RESULTS
------------
Symptom validity testing, self-report and observer-report measures, and any cognitive testing are summarized in the Test Results section. The examiner is mindful that no single test establishes or rules out ADHD.

SYMPTOM VALIDITY
----------------
Results of symptom validity testing and any embedded validity indicators. Given the rate of symptom exaggeration in evaluations of adult ADHD, validity assessment is a standard part of this evaluation.

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS
----------------------
Mood and anxiety disorders that can mimic attentional complaints
Learning disorders
Sleep disorders
Substance use disorders
Medical conditions affecting attention
Normal variation under stress or sleep deprivation

DIAGNOSTIC IMPRESSION
---------------------
Application of the DSM-5-TR criteria to the data gathered. Each criterion is addressed and supported by specific evidence. Presentation (combined, predominantly inattentive, predominantly hyperactive-impulsive) and severity are specified when a diagnosis is offered.
