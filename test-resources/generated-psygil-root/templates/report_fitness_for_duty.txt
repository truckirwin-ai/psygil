REFERRAL QUESTION
-----------------
The referring employer requested a fitness for duty evaluation of {{PATIENT_NAME}} following concerns about the employee's psychological ability to perform the essential functions of the position safely and effectively. The evaluation is conducted consistent with the IACP Psychological Services Section guidelines and relevant ADA requirements.

NOTICE OF LIMITS ON CONFIDENTIALITY
-----------------------------------
The employee was informed that this is an employer-requested evaluation, that information obtained will be included in a written report provided to the employer, that the scope of disclosure is limited to information directly relevant to fitness for duty, and that the usual rules of doctor-patient confidentiality do not apply. The employee acknowledged understanding and voluntarily agreed to participate.

PROCEDURES
----------
The following procedures were used in the preparation of this report:
Review of records provided by the referring party
Clinical interview with the evaluee
Mental status examination
Collateral interview(s) where appropriate
Standardized psychological testing (see Test Results)
Symptom validity and effort testing
Review of position description and essential job functions
Review of the triggering incident(s) or reported concerns

RECORDS REVIEWED
----------------
Position description and essential functions
Incident reports, internal investigations, and performance documentation provided by the employer
Medical and mental health records authorized by the employee
Any prior fitness evaluations

RELEVANT BACKGROUND
-------------------
Employment history and length of service
Training, certifications, and disciplinary history
Medical, psychiatric, and substance use history
Family, social, and financial stressors relevant to the referral question

TRIGGERING CONCERNS
-------------------
Summary of the concerns raised by the employer, with specific incidents and dates where available. The examinee's account of each incident is summarized separately.

MENTAL STATUS EXAMINATION
-------------------------
Appearance, behavior, cooperation, speech, mood, affect, thought process and content, perception, cognition, insight, and judgment.

TEST RESULTS
------------
Psychological test results are summarized in the Test Results section. Test selection is guided by the referral question and by the essential functions of the position.

JOB-RELATED FUNCTIONAL ANALYSIS
-------------------------------
Psychological capacity to perform each essential function of the position, including judgment under stress, firearms capability where applicable, interpersonal functioning with the public and colleagues, and ability to tolerate the emotional and physical demands of the role.

CLINICAL FORMULATION
--------------------
Integration of background, clinical observations, test results, and functional analysis. Any diagnosis offered is the examiner's own determination based on DSM-5-TR criteria. The formulation addresses whether a mental condition is present and, if so, how it relates to the employee's ability to perform the essential functions of the job.

OPINION
-------
To a reasonable degree of psychological certainty, the examiner offers one of the following opinions: Fit for Duty; Fit for Duty with Accommodations; Temporarily Unfit, with reasonable expectation of restoration; or Unfit. The rationale ties directly to the clinical findings and the essential job functions.

RECOMMENDATIONS
---------------
Treatment recommendations, if a clinical condition is identified
Accommodations to enable return to duty, if applicable
Reasonable period before re-evaluation
Limitations on scope of disclosure to protect the employee's private medical information
