REFERRAL QUESTION

The referring party requested an evaluation of {{PATIENT_NAME}}'s risk for future violence, the risk factors contributing to that risk, and recommended risk management strategies. HISTORICAL RISK FACTORS
-----------------------
Prior violence and its characteristics
Age at first violent act
Relationship and employment instability
Substance use problems

CLINICAL RISK FACTORS
---------------------
Current symptoms of major mental illness
Lack of insight into illness and risk
Negative attitudes supportive of violence
Active symptoms consistent with the person's prior violent episodes
Current substance use
Response to treatment and engagement in care

RISK MANAGEMENT FACTORS
-----------------------
Feasibility of plans the examinee has made
Exposure to destabilizers (e.g., substances, high-risk environments, interpersonal conflict)
Availability and acceptability of personal supports
Likelihood of compliance with treatment and supervision
Stressors identified for the anticipated setting

FORMULATION
-----------
Integration of historical, clinical, and risk-management factors into a coherent account of how, when, and to whom the examinee poses risk. This includes identification of the primary drivers of risk and the circumstances under which risk is expected to rise or fall.

SCENARIO PLANNING
-----------------
Most likely scenario for future violence, including who, where, when, and how
Most severe plausible scenario
Conditions under which each scenario becomes more or less likely

SUMMARY RISK JUDGMENT
---------------------
Overall categorical judgment of risk (low, moderate, high) for the specified time frame and setting, with explicit reference to the risk factors and scenarios above. The judgment explicitly addresses the time horizon and setting requested by the referring party.

RISK MANAGEMENT RECOMMENDATIONS
-------------------------------
Monitoring strategies
Treatment interventions, including medication, psychotherapy, and specialty programs
Supervision conditions
Victim safety planning, where applicable
Recommended re-evaluation interval
