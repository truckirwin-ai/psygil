Diagnostics

Formulate the clinical picture. Every decision is yours; Psygil just keeps the paper trail tidy.

The differential grid

The Diagnostics tab renders candidate diagnoses drawn from the testing and interview record. For each candidate you can: mark it as supported, ruled out, deferred, or add a note. Each decision writes an immutable row in the audit log.

The four Final Formulation sections

  1. Clinical Impressions. Your formulation in your words.
  2. Ruled Out. What you considered and excluded, with reasoning.
  3. Validity and Effort. Validity scales, behavioral observations of effort, and conclusions about test profile credibility.
  4. Prognosis and Recommendations. Treatment, placement, or forensic recommendations as appropriate.

Save and Approve

Each section has a Save button and an Approve button next to its status chip.

  • Save, persists your draft. The chip shows Drafted.
  • Approve, marks the section as clinically signed. The chip shows Approved and the section collapses.
  • If you edit an approved section, approval is revoked automatically; you must approve again.

Gate 2 and the attestation

Once all four sections are approved, Gate 2 is satisfied. Tick the attestation checkbox on the Report Gates panel to confirm that the formulation is your own clinical judgment. That enables the Approve and Build Report button.

Why the ceremony? Your report will be read by attorneys, courts, and opposing experts. The attestation and approval chain is what your audit trail rides on.
