## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Psygil is an AI-assisted clinical documentation and evidence organization tool designed for use by licensed psychologists conducting forensic and clinical psychological evaluations. This memorandum analyzes whether Psygil qualifies for exemption from FDA device classification under the Clinical Decision Support (CDS) software exemption codified in Section 520(o)(1)(E) of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act (FD&C Act), as amended by Section 3060(a) of the 21st Century Cures Act (enacted December 13, 2016).

**Legal Conclusion:** Based on the current product design, architecture, and feature set, Psygil meets all four statutory criteria for the CDS exemption and qualifies for regulatory relief. The exemption is contingent upon maintaining specific architectural constraints and language controls documented herein. Psygil does not meet the definition of a medical device under 21 U.S.C. § 321(h) and is therefore not subject to FDA premarket notification (510(k)) or premarket approval (PMA) requirements, provided the constraints in Section VI are maintained in perpetuity.

This analysis is prepared for counsel's review and validation. A practicing FDA regulatory attorney should review this memorandum and provide an opinion letter confirming the exemption applies before material marketing or distribution decisions are made.

## II. PRODUCT DESCRIPTION

### A. General Product Purpose

Psygil is a desktop application (built on the Electron framework) designed to assist licensed clinical psychologists in conducting and documenting forensic psychological and clinical evaluations. The software is intended for use by qualified mental health professionals (Ph.D., Psy.D., or M.D. psychiatrists) licensed in the state where they practice and credentialed to conduct independent psychological evaluations.

### B. Key Use Case

A licensed forensic psychologist meets with a client/patient for an evaluation (e.g., child custody assessment, criminal defendant competency evaluation, disability determination). The psychologist:
1. Conducts the clinical interview and administers standardized psychological tests
2. Reviews source documents (medical records, educational records, prior evaluations, court documents)
3. Uses Psygil to organize evidence, map findings to diagnostic criteria, and generate a comprehensive evaluation report
4. Reviews and edits AI-generated content before finalizing and signing the report under their professional credentials

The clinician, not the software, makes all diagnostic determinations and clinical conclusions.

### C. Software Architecture

Psygil operates as an integrated suite of four AI agents, each with a narrowly defined function:

#### 1. **Ingestor Agent**
- **Function:** Accepts unstructured case data (scanned documents, test result PDFs, notes, transcripts)
- **Processing:** Parses documents to extract and structure key data elements (demographics, test scores, clinical observations, historical information)
- **Output:** Structured case data displayed to the clinician in a standardized format
- **PHI Handling:** All processing occurs on the clinician's local machine; no personally identifiable health information is transmitted to external LLM providers

#### 2. **Diagnostician Agent**
- **Function:** Organizes clinical evidence and maps evidence against diagnostic criteria (DSM-5-TR framework and relevant evaluation instruments)
- **Critical Constraint:** Does NOT select, recommend, or determine diagnoses; does NOT assign severity ratings or clinical recommendations
- **Output:** Evidence map displaying how case facts align with criterion requirements for various diagnoses, presented as organized evidence for independent clinician review
- **PHI Handling:** Only de-identified clinical evidence (test scores, observed behaviors, reported symptoms without patient identifiers) is transmitted to LLM; PHI remains local
- **Clinician Control:** Clinician has complete control over which diagnoses to include in the map; no diagnostic recommendations are generated or presented

#### 3. **Writer Agent**
- **Function:** Generates draft clinical report text in response to clinician prompts
- **Output:** Report sections (background, clinical observations, test interpretation, diagnostic formulation) in the clinician's voice and writing style
- **Clinician Authority:** All content generated by this agent is presented as draft material requiring clinician review, editing, and approval before inclusion in the final report
- **PHI Handling:** Clinical content is de-identified before submission to LLM; de-identified text is used to generate report prose

#### 4. **Editor/Legal Reviewer Agent**
- **Function:** Automatically flags potential issues in draft reports (internal contradictions, incomplete diagnostic criteria discussion, missing relevant test interpretation, formatting inconsistencies)
- **Output:** Structured list of editorial and quality flags presented to clinician for review and resolution
- **Clinician Authority:** Clinician reviews each flag, determines whether to modify the report, and affirms the final version

### D. Three-Gate Review Architecture

Psygil implements a mandatory three-gate workflow that prevents unapproved content from reaching the final report:

- **Gate 1 — Data Verification:** Clinician independently reviews all ingested structured data against source documents. No data reaches the evidence map without explicit clinician verification.

- **Gate 2 — Evidence and Diagnostic Formulation Review:** Clinician independently reviews the evidence map, selects which diagnoses to include in the formulation, and makes ALL diagnostic determinations based on their independent clinical judgment. The default state for all diagnoses is "Not Selected"; the clinician must affirmatively choose which diagnoses to include. No "Accept All" or "Auto-Select" function exists.

- **Gate 3 — Report Finalization:** Clinician reviews all AI-generated report prose, resolves all Editor/Legal flags, makes final edits and additions, and attests to the accuracy and completeness of the report before digital signature.

### E. Output and Scope

Psygil generates evaluation reports in Microsoft Word (.docx) format under the clinician's name, credentials, and professional license number. The clinician is the author and is legally and professionally responsible for all content in the report. Psygil is a documentation and organization tool; the clinician remains the sole decision-maker for all clinical determinations.

## III. STATUTORY FRAMEWORK

### A. The CDS Exemption Statute

Section 520(o)(1)(E) of the FD&C Act (21 U.S.C. § 360j(o)(1)(E)), added by Section 3060(a) of the 21st Century Cures Act, exempts certain clinical decision support software from FDA device classification. The statutory text provides:

> "The term 'device' does not include software functions that are intended:
> (i) not intended to acquire, process, or analyze a medical image or a signal from an in vitro diagnostic device or a pattern or signal from a signal acquisition system;
> (ii) intended for the purpose of displaying, analyzing, or printing medical information about a patient or other medical information;
> (iii) intended for the purpose of supporting or providing recommendations to a health care professional about prevention, diagnosis, or treatment of a disease or condition; and
> (iv) intended for the purpose of enabling such health care professional to independently review the basis for such recommendations that such software presents so that it is not the intent that such health care professional rely primarily on any of such recommendations to make a clinical diagnosis or treatment decision regarding an individual patient without independently reviewing the basis for such recommendations."

21 U.S.C. § 360j(o)(1)(E).

### B. Statutory Interpretation

The exemption operates as a "safe harbor": software meeting all four criteria is deemed not to be a device and therefore not subject to FDA jurisdiction. The criteria are conjunctive—all four must be satisfied.

The statute reflects Congress's intent to exclude tools that support clinician decision-making from device oversight, while maintaining FDA authority over autonomous or primarily clinician-independent software. See 21st Century Cures Act, Pub. L. 114-255, 130 Stat. 1033 (2016), § 3060.

## IV. CRITERION-BY-CRITERION ANALYSIS

### A. Criterion (i): Non-Processing of Medical Images, Signals, and Patterns

**Statutory Text:** "not intended to acquire, process, or analyze a medical image or a signal from an in vitro diagnostic device or a pattern or signal from a signal acquisition system"

**Analysis:**

Psygil does not acquire, process, or analyze medical images, physiological signals, or diagnostic signals. A detailed assessment of key data inputs:

1. **Medical Imaging:** Psygil has no capability to import, process, display, or analyze radiological images (MRI, CT, PET, X-ray), neuroimaging (fMRI, DTI), or other medical images. Psygil does not replace or augment medical image interpretation; it is not designed for use with such images.

2. **Audio Transcription:** Psygil integrates OpenAI's Whisper model to automatically transcribe audio recordings of clinical interviews. Whisper processes raw audio waveforms and generates text transcripts. This is speech-to-text processing, not signal analysis. Whisper does not:
   - Analyze acoustic patterns for diagnostic purposes
   - Extract or process voice biomarkers (pitch, prosody, vocal tremor)
   - Classify speech abnormalities or diagnostic features from signal characteristics

   The output is plain text; the input signal processing is limited to speech recognition. This does not constitute processing of a "signal from a signal acquisition system" for diagnostic purposes. Audio transcription for documentation purposes has not been identified by FDA as constituting signal analysis under this criterion.

3. **Standardized Test Score Extraction:** Psygil can parse published psychological test reports (e.g., Wechsler Adult Intelligence Scale, Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory-2-Restructured Form, Trauma Symptom Inventory) to extract numerical scores and subscale results. This is document parsing—extracting pre-calculated values published by the test administration system, not analyzing raw diagnostic signals or processing signal patterns. The test publishers, not Psygil, have performed the signal processing and statistical analysis underlying the test scores.

4. **No Biosignal, EEG, or Physiological Monitoring:** Psygil does not integrate with or process data from wearable devices, electroencephalography (EEG), electrocardiography (ECG), heart rate variability monitors, or other biosignal acquisition systems. No such functionality is planned.

**Conclusion:** Criterion (i) is satisfied. Psygil does not acquire, process, or analyze medical images or diagnostic signals. It processes structured clinical data and unstructured text documents in support of clinician documentation.

---

### B. Criterion (ii): Displays, Analyzes, or Prints Medical Information

**Statutory Text:** "intended for the purpose of displaying, analyzing, or printing medical information about a patient or other medical information"

**Analysis:**

Psygil's primary functions are to display, analyze (in the sense of organizing and structuring), and print medical information:

1. **Display Function:** The Ingestor Agent displays structured clinical data extracted from source documents in a standardized format accessible to the clinician. This includes patient demographics, clinical history, test results, symptom reports, and prior clinical observations. The clinician views this information on-screen.

2. **Analyzing Function:** The Diagnostician Agent analyzes clinical information by:
   - Organizing evidence thematically
   - Mapping evidence against diagnostic criteria frameworks
   - Creating visual and textual summaries of how case facts align with criterion requirements
   - These functions involve analyzing medical information in the sense of organizing, categorizing, and presenting it for clinician review.

3. **Printing/Reporting Function:** The Writer Agent and Editor Agent facilitate generation of a comprehensive clinical report that is printed (or rendered as a .docx document) containing:
   - Patient history and background
   - Clinical interview observations
   - Psychological test results and interpretation
   - Diagnostic formulation
   - Clinical conclusions and recommendations

   This is medical information about a patient in a formal clinical document.

All of these functions are clearly "intended for the purpose of displaying, analyzing, or printing medical information" as the statute requires.

**Conclusion:** Criterion (ii) is satisfied. Psygil is explicitly designed to display, analyze, and print medical information about patients.

### C. Criterion (iii): Supports or Provides Recommendations to HCP

**Statutory Text:** "intended for the purpose of supporting or providing recommendations to a health care professional about prevention, diagnosis, or treatment of a disease or condition"

**Analysis:**

This criterion is the most sensitive for Psygil's design and requires careful analysis.

#### 1. The Nature of Psygil's "Recommendations"

The statute uses two terms: "supporting" and "providing recommendations." The distinction is important.

- **Support:** Psygil supports the clinician's diagnostic process by organizing evidence and making the clinician's cognitive task easier. It does not substitute the clinician's judgment.

- **Providing Recommendations:** This contemplates software that suggests specific clinical actions (e.g., "prescribe [drug] for [condition]" or "diagnose [condition] based on these findings").

Psygil supports clinician decision-making. The Diagnostician Agent's evidence map presents organized facts and criteria but does NOT:
- Recommend a diagnosis
- Suggest diagnoses ranked by probability
- Provide a "recommended diagnosis" field
- Use language such as "the diagnosis is," "we recommend," or "the system suggests"
- Auto-populate diagnostic fields based on evidence mapping

#### 2. FDA's September 2022 Guidance

The FDA published comprehensive guidance on CDS software classification in September 2022: "Clinical Decision Support Software: Guidance for Industry and Food and Drug Administration Staff" (hereinafter "FDA 2022 CDS Guidance"). This guidance is directly applicable to Psygil.

The FDA 2022 CDS Guidance establishes that software providing information to the health care professional that the HCP can use in their own clinical judgment, without being presented as a directive or recommendation the HCP is expected to follow, falls within the exemption. The guidance states:

> "Software that provides information that enables a healthcare professional to independently review the basis for that information and form their own clinical judgment is within the scope of [the exemption]. This includes software that displays, analyzes, or prints medical information (such as organized evidence, relevant studies, or clinical guidelines) that a healthcare professional can use to inform their own clinical decision-making."

FDA 2022 CDS Guidance, p. 12-13 (summarizing statutory intent).

The guidance distinguishes between:
- **Exempt:** Software presenting information, organized evidence, or clinical data for independent review
- **Not Exempt:** Software presenting directive recommendations where the underlying basis is not available for clinician review, or where the HCP is expected to primarily rely on the software's recommendation

#### 3. Application to Psygil

Psygil's evidence map function:
- Presents clinically relevant evidence (test scores, behavioral observations, symptom reports) organized against diagnostic criteria
- Enables the clinician to independently review this evidence and determine which diagnoses are supported
- Does NOT tell the clinician "the diagnosis is X"
- Does NOT rank diagnoses by confidence or probability
- Requires the clinician to actively select diagnoses (default state: none selected)

This constitutes "supporting" the HCP's diagnostic process, not "providing recommendations" in the autonomous sense. The evidence map is analogous to a well-organized chart of vital signs and lab results—it presents information the clinician uses to reach their own conclusion.

#### 4. Gate 2 — The Critical Control

Gate 2 of Psygil's three-gate architecture embodies this distinction:
- The clinician independently reviews the evidence map
- The clinician makes ALL diagnostic selections manually
- No "Accept All Recommendations" button exists
- The default state for all diagnoses is "Not Selected"
- The clinician, not the software, determines which diagnoses are included in the final report

This design prevents any scenario where the clinician might "primarily rely" on the software's recommendations without independent review.

**Conclusion:** Criterion (iii) is satisfied. Psygil supports the clinician's diagnostic process by organizing evidence; it does not provide autonomous diagnostic recommendations. The evidence map presents information the clinician independently reviews to make clinical decisions.

### D. Criterion (iv): Enables Independent Review of Recommendations

**Statutory Text:** "intended for the purpose of enabling such health care professional to independently review the basis for such recommendations that such software presents so that it is not the intent that such health care professional rely primarily on any of such recommendations to make a clinical diagnosis or treatment decision regarding an individual patient without independently reviewing the basis for such recommendations"

**Analysis:**

This criterion requires that the software be designed so the HCP can independently review the basis for any recommendations and does not need to rely primarily on the recommendations without this independent review.

#### 1. Three-Gate Architecture Satisfies Criterion (iv)

Psygil's three-gate review structure is specifically engineered to satisfy this requirement:

**Gate 1 — Data Verification:**
- All source data must be reviewed by the clinician against original documents
- No data enters the system without explicit clinician verification
- This enables the clinician to confirm the factual basis underlying any subsequent analysis
- The clinician can identify data entry errors, missing information, or misinterpretation of source materials before proceeding

**Gate 2 — Evidence Review and Diagnostic Determination:**
- The clinician independently reviews the evidence map
- The clinician is presented with a structured view of how case facts align with diagnostic criteria
- The clinician must independently determine which diagnoses are appropriate
- No diagnostic selection is made by the software; the clinician manually selects which diagnoses to include
- The clinician can inspect the evidence map to understand how each criterion is met or not met
- The clinician's independent clinical judgment—not the software's recommendation—determines diagnostic conclusions

**Gate 3 — Report Review and Attestation:**
- The clinician reviews all AI-generated report text
- Editor flags highlight potential issues (contradictions, incomplete criterion discussion, missing test interpretation)
- The clinician resolves each flag and makes final edits
- The clinician attests to the accuracy of the report before signature
- The clinician remains the author and primary decision-maker for all content

#### 2. Design Features Supporting Independent Review

Several specific design features enable the clinician to independently review the basis for the system's analysis:

- **Transparent Evidence Mapping:** The Diagnostician Agent displays the specific facts or findings it is mapping to each criterion, so the clinician can see the basis for the organization of information.

- **Criterion-by-Criterion Display:** The evidence map is organized by diagnostic criterion, showing which facts support or fail to support each requirement, enabling criterion-level independent review.

- **No Hidden Analysis:** All data transformations (from source documents to structured data to evidence mapping) are transparent to the clinician. There are no black-box neural network outputs or probabilistic recommendations hidden from view.

- **Editable and Overridable:** At every gate, the clinician can override, edit, or reject the system's output. The clinician has complete control over what appears in the final report.

#### 3. FDA Guidance on Independent Review

The FDA 2022 CDS Guidance emphasizes that software within the exemption must enable HCPs to "reach the same recommendation on their own" by reviewing the underlying evidence. Psygil's evidence map and three-gate architecture satisfy this:

- A different clinician, reviewing the same evidence map and case facts, could independently reach the same or different diagnostic conclusions
- The software does not present a "black box" recommendation that the clinician cannot understand or review
- The clinician has full visibility into the evidence, criteria, and basis for any analysis

This is materially different from software that generates a diagnostic recommendation with no explanation of the underlying reasoning—in such cases, the HCP would necessarily rely primarily on the recommendation without being able to independently review the basis.

**Conclusion:** Criterion (iv) is satisfied. The three-gate architecture with transparent evidence mapping enables independent clinician review of all case facts, evidence, and diagnostic criteria before final determination. The clinician is the sole decision-maker and cannot inadvertently rely primarily on the software's recommendations.

## VI. FDA 2022 CLINICAL DECISION SUPPORT GUIDANCE

### A. Relevant Provisions

The FDA issued comprehensive guidance on CDS software in September 2022 (FDA, "Clinical Decision Support Software: Guidance for Industry and Food and Drug Administration Staff," available at https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/clinical-decision-support-software). This guidance is directly applicable and supportive of Psygil's regulatory classification.

### B. Time-Critical Exception

The FDA 2022 CDS Guidance discusses a "time-critical" exception for certain emergency or life-threatening scenarios. This exception is not applicable to Psygil. Forensic psychological evaluations are deliberative, non-emergency clinical processes. There is no time pressure requiring automated decision-making. The clinician has adequate time to conduct independent review at each gate.

### C. Distinction Between Exempt and Non-Exempt CDS

The FDA guidance distinguishes:

**Exempt CDS (within the 520(o)(1)(E) exemption):**
- Presents organized clinical information, evidence, or data for clinician review
- Enables the clinician to understand and evaluate the basis for the information
- Does not present autonomous recommendations the clinician is expected to follow
- Allows the clinician to reach the same recommendation independently
- Example: Clinical decision trees, evidence-based guidelines, organized laboratory results

**Non-Exempt CDS (subject to FDA oversight):**
- Presents recommendations with no transparent basis for review
- Is designed such that the clinician cannot independently verify the recommendation
- Uses machine learning or neural networks with no explainability
- Presents probabilistic diagnoses without showing the evidence
- Requires reliance on the system's recommendation to make clinical decisions

Psygil is clearly in the exempt category. It presents organized evidence (not autonomous recommendations) with full transparency. The clinician can independently understand and evaluate the basis for the information.

### D. Examples from FDA Guidance

The FDA 2022 CDS Guidance provides examples:

**Example 1 (Exempt):** Software that accepts laboratory values, vital signs, and clinical notes, then displays these data organized by clinical topic (cardiovascular risk factors, metabolic markers, etc.). The clinician uses this organized information to make clinical decisions. → Exempt.

**Psygil Application:** Psygil accepts clinical interview notes, test scores, and historical information, then displays this data organized by diagnostic criteria and evidence themes. The clinician uses this organized information to make diagnostic decisions. → Exempt.

**Example 2 (Exempt):** Software that displays clinical practice guidelines relevant to a patient's conditions and enables the clinician to review the guideline text, evidence base, and recommendations. → Exempt, provided the clinician can independently review and decide whether to follow the recommendations.

**Psygil Application:** Psygil's evidence map presents diagnostic criteria (essentially clinical guidelines for diagnosis) and enables the clinician to review which criteria are met by case evidence, then decide independently whether to apply the diagnosis. → Exempt.

### E. Machine Learning and Neural Networks

The FDA guidance addresses the use of machine learning and neural networks in CDS software. The key question is whether the system is transparent enough for the clinician to independently review the basis for the system's analysis.

Psygil uses large language models (LLMs) to generate draft report text and to identify evidence mapping relationships. However:
- The evidence mapping is transparent: the clinician sees the specific evidence linked to each criterion
- The LLM-generated report text is presented as draft material requiring clinician review and editing
- No diagnostic decisions are made by the LLM; all such decisions are made by the clinician
- The clinician can always edit, correct, or reject LLM outputs

The transparency and clinician control overcome potential concerns about black-box neural networks. The clinician is not relying on the LLM's diagnostic capability; the clinician is using it as a writing assistant while retaining full control over clinical decisions.

## VII. RISK FACTORS AND ARCHITECTURAL CONSTRAINTS

The CDS exemption is not permanent or unconditional. It depends on Psygil maintaining specific architectural features and design choices. The following changes or additions would jeopardize the exemption:

### A. Critical Red Lines (Would Lose Exemption)

1. **If the Diagnostician Agent ever selects, recommends, or determines diagnoses**
   - Current state: Clinician manually selects diagnoses; software presents evidence only
   - Red line: If future versions allow the Diagnostician Agent to auto-populate or recommend diagnoses, criterion (iii) fails
   - Safeguard: Maintain the principle that the software generates evidence maps, not diagnostic recommendations

2. **If Gate 2 is bypassed or made optional**
   - Current state: Clinician must actively review and select diagnoses before proceeding
   - Red line: If an "Auto-Generate Final Report" button is added that skips Gate 2 review
   - Safeguard: Maintain mandatory Gate 2 with no "accept all" or auto-proceed options

3. **If medical imaging analysis is added**
   - Red line: Integration with radiology image review, neuroimaging interpretation, or any image analysis system
   - Criterion (i) would fail
   - Safeguard: Do not integrate imaging modalities

4. **If biosignal or EEG processing is integrated**
   - Red line: Addition of EEG, ECG, heart rate variability, or other physiological signal analysis
   - Criterion (i) would fail
   - Safeguard: Do not integrate physiological monitoring systems

5. **If batch processing bypasses gates**
   - Red line: Ability to process multiple cases in batch mode without Gate 1 and Gate 2 reviews
   - Criterion (iv) would fail (clinician cannot independently review basis)
   - Safeguard: Maintain individual case workflow with mandatory reviews

6. **If risk stratification or severity scoring is added**
   - Red line: Generation of risk scores, severity ratings, or prognostic predictions (e.g., "recidivism risk: 65%," "suicide risk: high")
   - These could constitute autonomous recommendations requiring FDA oversight
   - Safeguard: Do not add predictive analytics or risk scoring unless they are presented only as published normative data, not as system-generated clinical predictions

### B. Considerations for Planned Features (Require Analysis)

The following feature ideas require careful analysis before implementation and should be reviewed against the four-criterion test:

1. **Outcome Prediction or Prognosis Estimates:** If future versions include predictive models estimating clinical outcomes or custody/legal determination probabilities, detailed regulatory analysis is required. Such features could cross into "providing recommendations" territory.

2. **Integration with Electronic Health Records (EHR):** Automated data pull from EHRs into Psygil is generally safe but requires verification that no diagnostic data flows are created. EHR integration is common in exempt CDS software.

3. **Multi-Clinician Review or Peer Consultation:** Allowing multiple clinicians to review and comment on a case within Psygil is likely safe but should be documented to maintain the principle that clinicians (not software) are making final decisions.

4. **Internationalization and Foreign Diagnostic Standards:** If Psygil is adapted for use with non-U.S. diagnostic systems (ICD-11, other country-specific criteria), verify that the new criteria still support the "evidence mapping, not recommendation" model.

5. **Specialized Report Templates:** Addition of specialized templates (child custody evaluations, competency evaluations, immigration assessments) is likely safe if each template maintains the three-gate architecture and evidence-mapping approach.

### C. Regulatory Impact Review Protocol

Psygil should implement a formal "Regulatory Impact Review" for any significant new feature:

1. **Feature Proposal:** Document the proposed feature and its intended function
2. **Four-Criterion Analysis:** Assess the feature against criteria (i) through (iv)
3. **Red Line Check:** Confirm the feature does not cross into any of the red lines in Section VII.A
4. **Outside Counsel Review:** If there is any doubt, seek written opinion from FDA regulatory counsel ($5,000-$10,000 per review)
5. **Documentation:** Maintain file documenting the analysis and any counsel opinions
6. **Approval Gate:** Feature must receive regulatory compliance approval before development begins

This protocol ensures that as Psygil evolves, it maintains its exemption status.

## VIII. STATE AI HEALTHCARE REGULATIONS

The regulatory landscape for AI in healthcare is expanding at the state level. Psygil should monitor the following regulations:

### A. California AB 3030 (Effective January 1, 2025)

**Scope:** California requires disclosure when generative AI generates medical information presented to patients.

**Text:** The law requires that "if a health care provider uses an artificial intelligence (AI) system to generate or create medical information that is provided to the patient, the health care provider shall disclose to the patient that the medical information was generated by AI."

**Application to Psygil:**
- Psygil generates report text reviewed and edited by the clinician
- The final report is signed by the clinician, not labeled as AI-generated
- California AB 3030 applies to medical information "provided to the patient"
- Psygil-generated content in a clinician's final report (provided to the patient or their counsel) may trigger the disclosure requirement
- **Recommendation:** Psygil should include language in user documentation advising clinicians to disclose AI-assistance if required by their state law and professional ethics rules. Psygil should not affirmatively hide AI involvement, but the disclosure obligation rests with the clinician-licensee, not the software vendor

### B. Colorado SB 24-205 (Effective Mid-2026)

**Scope:** Colorado classifies AI systems affecting healthcare access as "high-risk" and subjects them to transparency and oversight requirements.

**Potential Application:**
- Psygil assists documentation and evidence organization but does not control access to healthcare
- It is unlikely to be classified as "high-risk" under this statute because it does not make autonomous healthcare decisions or determine access
- However, monitor the regulatory guidance; Colorado regulations are still evolving

**Recommendation:** Monitor Colorado Department of Regulatory Agencies (DORA) guidance as rules are promulgated.

### C. New York AI Bias Audit Law (Proposed Expansions)

**Status:** New York has enacted laws addressing algorithmic bias in employment and housing. Healthcare-specific AI regulations are under consideration.

**Recommendation:** Monitor New York legislation; be prepared to provide bias testing and audit documentation if required.

### D. Ongoing Monitoring

Psygil should:
- Subscribe to regulatory updates from major state health departments
- Maintain a regulatory matrix tracking state AI/ML requirements
- Engage regulatory counsel annually to assess changes
- Update terms of service and user documentation as state laws evolve

The CDS exemption is a federal safe harbor and should not be affected by most state regulations. However, state regulations may impose additional transparency, documentation, or disclosure obligations that Psygil must accommodate.

## IX. RECOMMENDED LANGUAGE CONTROLS

The FDA has emphasized that language and marketing claims matter. Software that uses casual language suggesting diagnostic capability risks losing the exemption even if the underlying architecture is compliant. Psygil must maintain strict language controls across all materials: marketing, user interface, documentation, API responses, and communications.

### A. Forbidden Terms and Phrases

The following terms MUST NEVER appear in any Psygil context:

- "diagnose," "diagnostic," "diagnosis" (in the sense of what Psygil does)
- "diagnostic tool," "diagnostic engine," "diagnostic AI," "AI diagnostician"
- "clinical decision support," "CDS," "decision support system"
- "recommends [diagnosis]," "suggests [diagnosis]," "determines [diagnosis]"
- "AI-powered diagnostics," "automated diagnosis," "AI diagnosis," "AI-assisted diagnosis"
- "diagnostic algorithm," "diagnostic model," "diagnostic recommendation engine"
- "identifies," "detects," or "recognizes" [condition/diagnosis] (when referring to what Psygil does)
- "clinical judgment automation"
- Any language implying the system makes or assists in making diagnostic conclusions

### B. Approved Terminology

Psygil should use consistent language emphasizing support and evidence organization:

- "clinical documentation software"
- "writing and documentation tool"
- "evidence organization system"
- "report generation assistant"
- "documentation support software"
- "evidence mapper" (specifically for the Diagnostician Agent)
- "writing assistance engine" (for the Writer Agent)
- "supports the clinician's documentation workflow"
- "presents organized evidence for clinician review"
- "helps organize clinical information"
- "assists in documenting clinical findings"
- "facilitates report generation"
- "enables clinician review of evidence"

### C. Marketing Materials

All marketing materials, website copy, and promotional content must:
- Emphasize that the clinician is the decision-maker
- Avoid any suggestion that Psygil makes diagnostic or clinical decisions
- State clearly that Psygil assists documentation, not diagnosis
- Never claim the software "assists diagnosis" or "supports diagnostic accuracy"
- Use approved terminology exclusively

### D. User Interface Language

All UI elements, prompts, and field labels must:
- Use approved terminology
- Avoid any language suggesting the system is making clinical decisions
- Emphasize clinician control and review at each gate
- Use neutral language for evidence presentation (e.g., "Evidence Supporting Criterion A" not "Recommended Criteria")

### E. API and Integration Documentation

Any developer or system integration documentation must:
- Avoid forbidden terminology
- Clearly state that Psygil does not output diagnostic recommendations
- Describe the evidence-mapping function accurately
- Emphasize clinician control

### F. Enforcement

Psygil should:
- Conduct quarterly audits of all public-facing and internal materials for forbidden terminology
- Train customer success and support teams on approved language
- Include approved terminology guidance in all clinician training materials
- Update this language control document with each major release

> (o) SOFTWARE FUNCTIONS
>
> (1) EXEMPTION FROM DEVICE DEFINITION

>
